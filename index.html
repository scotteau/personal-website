About me

🎉Excellent! Time to know a bit about me.

Knowing a new person could be problem sometimes. I will keep it short. I was chef 🥘for more than 5 years, Studied Digital Design in Curtin University.I had my *own 👨‍🍳restaurant* for 3 years, had my highs and lows. 🤔After closing down the restaurant, I decided to pursue 👨🏻‍💻software engineering career since it is always something I wanted to do while in uni.

I drove 🕶🚙Uber while teach myself web development, it is an interesting phase of my life, because the job was very flexible and offered such unique social experience, i.e. meeting so many different people all with different narratives. On the other hand, I was picking up the web dev knowledge rather quickly as I had some foundation built in uni but the technology moved fast since I graduated, there are so many things to learn. It is 😱scary and 🤓exciting at the same time.

Anyway, back to me, I always consider myself as a 🤔pragmatic solver and very 😝authentic person. I take 💯responsibility of my life, and examine myself constantly and vigorously to eliminate any false thinking. After couple months of part-time study, I found myself struggling with how to write the production level code, and confused of which tech path to take. That's when I realised I must find guidance to navigate this technology career path, like fresh young chefs were told in the kitchen, "Don't be afraid to ask for help".

I enrolled in a Sydney-based ⌨️*coding boot camp* couple months later. I planned everything and was ready to be away for solid 3 months to really sharpen myself. The Covid-19 😷pandemic happened. Two days before my ✈️ flight to Sydney, the infested cases have been doubled since I last checked couple of days ago. "It is definitely getting worse" I thought. Now I have some decision to make, I don't think I should go to Sydney. But everything was organised and planned, it is a bit hard to walk away but it was the right decision to ignore the 🌊sunken cost and stay home. Two weeks later, the cases grew 20 folds, and all travel was ✋banned🙀.

😂Lucky for me, the school did offer online teaching. It is not something I had expected and I could not foresee any of this, but it is often crucial in life to find the serenity to accept the things cannot be changed, the courage to change the things that can and the wisdom to tell the difference. This kind of becomes my motto for life.

I was teaching myself Angular before, it had this so-called "steep learning curve". However , it did provide me exposure to so many technical topics and showed me the some of the best practices that improve my skills as a web developer.

The boot camp was really good too, we went through the basics, and I picked up React & Node.js along the way. Though I missed out on some of the social experience of a coding boot camp, It did offered me the chance the consolidate my learning, and helped me to obtain a deeper understanding of the craft of modern web development. It is also worth to mention, the most immense learning for me was to gain the firsthand experiences of how to work as a team on several real software projects. Prior to the school, I can't possible learn any of this since you can't really call yourself a team if you are the only player. I really believe this made me ready for the real deal, I will be fit right in on any software teams.😎

So yeah, here is my story. What are your takeaways? I hope they are good.😝
